  <div class="poem-meta">Seagull: The Song of the Lake</div>

  <div class="poem-grid">
    <!-- 中文栏 -->
    <div class="poem-col">
      <div class="poem-lang-label">中文</div>
金鱼在水中滑翔
我却是只有空网的渔夫
我和你出海，伴着沉船
在你的怀抱中，我渐渐死去
你在南方，我是一只迷失的候鸟
任凭你的呼喝决定方向
你在北方，冰冻了我的翅膀
然后你亲吻我，起身离开
你把握着航行的方向
我只是一个迷路的小沙弥
美狄亚，你是我永远的追随
离了你我不知道如何生存
你在南方，我是一只迷失的候鸟
任凭你的呼喝决定方向
你在北方，冰冻了我的翅膀
然后你亲吻了我，起身离去
    </div>

    <!-- 英文栏 -->
    <div class="poem-col">
      <div class="poem-lang-label">English</div>
The goldfish glides through the water;
I am only a fisherman with an empty net.
I set out to sea with you, among the sinking ships,
and in your arms I slowly die.
When you are in the South, I am a lost migratory bird,
drifting wherever your command directs.
When you are in the North, my wings are frozen,
and then you kiss me, rise, and leave.
You hold the course of the voyage;
I am merely a wandering novice monk.
Medea, you are the fate I forever follow—
without you I do not know how to live.
When you are in the South, I am a lost migratory bird,
drifting wherever your command directs.
When you are in the North, my wings are frozen,
and then you kiss me again, rise, and depart.

    <div class="poem-col">
      <div class="poem-lang-label">English</div>
Softly,  
you gaze at me—  
as gently as spring water looks upon light.  
O your gentleness,  
the scattering of loosened feathers,  
restless, restless,  
and gentle,  
unfolding between your brows.  

Wings fall apart in tender strokes—  
touching, touching—  
glancing toward the thresholds of light and shadow,  
touching,  
spreading like water made of tenderness.  
O your gentleness,  
congealing,  
a feather stiffening inside my blood,  
held in a dim, trembling light.  

O you water-like one,  
looking at me as water looks on.  
Between the pores beneath my feet,  
in everything I see,  
everywhere—  
your water-like gentleness,  
your water-like gaze  
stills my eternity.  

The galloping horse,  
its falling feathers melt into gentleness.  
No—only he knows,  
only he knows my futile heart.  
O your gentleness,  
only he knows.

I know no sleep at night;  
none in humankind knows sleep.  
Within your gentleness,  
I cannot bear to close my eyes;  
I cannot bear to open them.  
O your breath—  
pure, beautiful;  
O you, sleeping in gentleness—  
unstained, joyful.  

I weep inside that gentleness,  
burning,  
quiet,  
fading into rest within your tenderness,  
and weeping in that rest.  

No one born of flesh may sleep;  
nor can I sleep in your embrace.  
O your gentleness  
stroking me,  
stroking me in dreams,  
a Maya-like caress.  

I am displaced,  
crying in the meadow.  
Love—  
whirling in confusion,  
turning in a waltz.  
O your gentleness,  
I linger inside love,  
retrieve myself among feathers,  
clear and translucent,  
rising inside rain,  
falling, spilling—  
a suffocating gleam of the eye.  

Your tender face,  
the one I adore,  
the one that touches me,  
makes my heart tremble,  
the one that sleeps—  
and the one I depart from in sorrow.

As if departing in sorrow,  
spring water rests in gentleness.  
I am intoxicated,  
lost in inversion.  
In my eyes there is only you.  

Among the troubled crowd,  
like revelation,  
I watch from the tower-top—  
your purity,  
your serenity.  

I love in rapture,  
sing beside the nightingale,  
blossom inside the rose,  
drift fragrant before the lilac.  

Pale-violet feathers—  
Maya, Maya—  
and dreams of rose-pink.  
O this gentleness,  
I touch it,  
jumping between my fingers.  

I have never seen a lover with my eyes.  
Do not forget to sing;  
do not cease singing.  
Where shall I go to find it?  

Turning, turning—  
retrieving a single thread  
lit within candlelight,  
flickering.  

Water poured into sleep,  
overflowing in dreams.  
Never have my eyes seen—  
O your gentleness,  
small-bird-like.  

Why should I fall silent before you?  
I would rather wander,  
lost inside your barrenness.  

The heat upon my lips—  
how many nights has it burned?  
How many layers  
of returning,  
circling through the human realm?  

A suffocating love.  
O the fragrance of grapes,  
leaves unfolding,  
the soul turning.  

My eyes—  
your face blurred;  
my lips—  
my heart intoxicated;  
my hands—  
touching the long stillness.  

I gaze,  
from the tower-top at the starlight.  
O your gentleness—  
why not circle among planets,  
why not dissolve in moonlight?  
Where is there no moon?  

O you water-like one—  
restless, restless,  
wordless, silent.  

I seek you vainly,  
unaware,  
for you never asked me to wait.  
Wordless waiting,  
I sigh in waiting,  
sigh and weep—  
weep inside your gentleness,  
inside your embrace.  

You sleep in silence;  
I rest in quiet.  

O that gentleness—  
when shall I possess it?  
The promised gentleness,  
gentleness like yours.  

I sigh softly,  
settling into the waves of moonlit water.  

Moonlight does not leave summer nights;  
the water’s far-off steps,  
far-off calling—  
O your gentleness.  

I gaze far in calling,  
and sleep in waiting.

    <div class="poem-col">
      <div class="poem-lang-label">English</div>

In sleep there is joy;  
and joy is mine—  
extinction, stillness.  
When shall I speak of myself?  
On which day shall I cling?  

Love whirls in rotation,  
a dream of violets—  
Maya, Maya.  
O you, beloved,  
when do you stand before my eyes,  
filling them so they see only you?  
In a velvet dream,  
waves rise with intoxicating fragrance.  
Pomegranates, crystal and dripping—  
a rich dawn laced with flame.  

O you, beloved—  
on what day did you come beside me?  
Your fragrance, pure and bright,  
makes me drunk,  
makes me drunk—  
lost within intoxication.

---

In sleep I feel joy;  
and joy is yours—  
embers fading, peaceful.  
A stream flows through the dream;  
you drift upon the water.  
I enter you,  
wander in summer’s sorrow.  
Where am I?  
Where am I?  

Snow drifts thick across the sky—  
how shall I follow your footprints?  
O my desolate one,  
where shall I seek?  
Why will you not love me?  
When did you cease to love me?  
When did you speak me into being?  

I appear—  
a pitiable little one,  
wrapped wholly in love,  
nursed by love’s fragrant milk.  

From my fingertips overflowing—  
I grasp those hands;  
in moonlight veiled,  
I gaze into those eyes.

---

O my joy—  
joy within this realm.  
Moonlight casts a clear shadow;  
sunlight reveals a graceful form—  
lovely,  
pliant,  
turning like a fantastical dream.  

When was there me?  
When am I myself?  
This patch of pure land—  
a realm of purity.  

So I pluck fruit to eat,  
cup clear spring to drink,  
joy in what is mine,  
joy in this single realm.
